Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models

Tom Brown, Benjamin Mann, Nick Ryder, Melanie Subbiah

Abstract
We investigate the scaling properties of neural language models and find that performance depends strongly on model size, dataset size, and compute. We derive power-law relationships that predict performance across orders of magnitude.

Key Contributions:
- Performance follows a power law in model parameters with exponent 0.076
- Optimal batch size scales linearly with model size
- Overfitting occurs when dataset size is insufficient relative to model parameters

Methods
We trained models ranging from 10^7 to 10^11 parameters on datasets from 10^9 to 10^12 tokens. We measured cross-entropy loss on held-out test sets.

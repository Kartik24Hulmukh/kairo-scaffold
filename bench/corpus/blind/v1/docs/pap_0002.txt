Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models

Tom Brown, Benjamin Mann, Nick Ryder, Melanie Subbiah

Abstract
We present a novel approach to scaling laws for neural language models. Our method demonstrates significant improvements over baseline approaches across multiple benchmarks.

Key Contributions:
- Our method achieves state-of-the-art results on SuperGLUE.
- We introduce a new architecture that reduces training time by 50%.
- Our approach generalizes to unseen domains without additional training.

Methodology
We use a transformer-based architecture with 34 layers and attention mechanisms.

Experimental Results
On the primary benchmark, our model achieves FID 2.03 and sFID 4.84. The precision score is 0.26.

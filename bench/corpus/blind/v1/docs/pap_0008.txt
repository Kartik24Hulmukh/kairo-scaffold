Toolformer: Language Models Can Teach Themselves to Use Tools

Timo Schick

Abstract
We present a novel approach to toolformer. Our method demonstrates significant improvements over baseline approaches across multiple benchmarks.

Key Claims:
1. Our model achieves 0.88 accuracy on the test set.
2. We show that the proposed method is more efficient than prior work.

Methodology
We use a transformer-based architecture with 24 layers and attention mechanisms.

Experimental Results
On the primary benchmark, our model achieves FID 3.66 and sFID 2.54. The precision score is 0.68.

Diffusion Models Beat GANs on Image Synthesis

Prafulla Dhariwal, Alex Nichol

Abstract
We show that diffusion models achieve image sample quality superior to current state-of-the-art generative models. Through comprehensive experiments, we demonstrate that diffusion models can generate higher-fidelity images than GANs across multiple datasets.

Main Results:
1. Our model achieves FID 2.45 on CIFAR-10, compared to 3.05 for the best GAN.
2. Diffusion models show better diversity metrics across all evaluated datasets.

Methodology
We use a U-Net architecture with attention mechanisms at each resolution. The model is trained using a weighted variational bound on the data likelihood.

Experimental Results
On ImageNet 128x128, our model achieves FID 2.27 and sFID 4.59. The precision score is 0.84 and recall is 0.53. See Figure 1 for sample images and Table 2 for quantitative comparisons.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks

Patrick Lewis, Ethan Perez

Abstract
We present a novel approach to retrieval-augmented generation for knowledge-intensive nlp tasks. Our method demonstrates significant improvements over baseline approaches across multiple benchmarks.

Main Results:
1. Our model achieves FID 4.31 on the primary benchmark.
2. Performance improves with scale following a power law.
3. The proposed method outperforms all baselines by a significant margin.

Methodology
We use a transformer-based architecture with 18 layers and attention mechanisms.

Experimental Results
On the primary benchmark, our model achieves FID 2.41 and sFID 2.95. The precision score is 0.64.

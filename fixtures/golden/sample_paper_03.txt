Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks
Patrick Lewis, Ethan Perez, Aleksandara Piktus, Fabio Petroni, Vladimir Karpukhin, Naman Goyal, Heinrich Küttler, Mike Lewis, Wen-tau Yih, Tim Rocktäschel, Sebastian Riedel, Douwe Kiela

Abstract:
We introduce Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) models, which combine pre-trained parametric memory with non-parametric memory (retrieved Wikipedia documents) to improve performance on knowledge-intensive NLP tasks.

Key Claims:
1. RAG models produce more factual, specific, and diverse responses than parametric-only models.
2. In-context retrieval improves answer accuracy on open-domain QA.

Methods:
We combine a dense passage retriever (DPR) with a sequence-to-sequence generator (BART). The retriever fetches documents from Wikipedia.

Results:
RAG achieves 44.5% exact match on Natural Questions.

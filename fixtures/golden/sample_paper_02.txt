LoRA: Low-Rank Adaptation of Large Language Models
Edward J. Hu, Yibin Shen, Phillip Wallis, Zeyuan Allen-Zhu, Yuanzhi Li, Shean Wang, Lu Wang, Weizhu Chen

Abstract:
We propose Low-Rank Adaptation, or LoRA, which freezes the pre-trained model weights and injects trainable rank decomposition matrices into each layer of the Transformer architecture, greatly reducing the number of trainable parameters for downstream tasks.

Key Claims:
1. LoRA reduces the number of trainable parameters by 10000 times.
2. Performance is comparable or superior to full fine-tuning.

Methods:
We parameterize the weight updates by representing them as product of two low-rank matrices. The rank r is set to 4 or 8 in our experiments.

Results:
LoRA achieves 82.5% on GLUE tasks with r=8. As reported in Table 1, trainable parameters are reduced to 0.12% of the original model.
